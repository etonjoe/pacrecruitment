QUALITY OF POST ABORTION CARE PROJECT NIGERIA

Quality of health care has been a core pillar of health systems reforms since the World Health Organization [WHO] publication titled, The world health report 2000: Health systems: Improving performance committed significant parts to the need to make health systems address usersâ€™ expectations. Generally framed as quality of health care, it is characterized as the extent to which health-care services provided to individuals and patient populations to advance looked-for health outcomes. We will draw on the seminal work of Donabedian, which provides three core-connected ingredients for assessing health care quality. These are (i) structure (facility infrastructure, management and staffing), (ii) process (technical/technical quality and patient experience) and (iii) outcomes (patient satisfaction, return visits and health outcomes).
Unsafe abortion remains one of the major causes of maternal mortality and morbidity in Nigeria. Tackling unsafe abortion is thus clearly a key to the achievement of SDGs 3 Target 1, as well as the promotion of health and wellbeing in Nigeria. An important first step to building sustainable change on SRHR issues rests on generating and using robust and contextual evidence to inform solutions to problems; midwifing enabling normative environments; fostering collaboration and networking around comprehensive SRHR services at scale; ensuring supportive advocacy; and expanding individual and relational agency. Our theory of change is that the delivery of quality PAC services can be catalyzed through the generation of a robust body of evidence that is used to rally SRHR networks and partnerships; build SRHR leadership, inform advocacy; and support capacity to identify, test, adapt, and scale up best and proven practices. Our study will provide robust and comparative evidence base on the situation of PAC services in Nigeria. By determining the state of PAC services, and clarifying barriers to the provision of quality PAC, we will equip a range of stakeholders, including providers with information, evidence, and capacity to address the situation in the country.
